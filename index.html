About Me

I received my B.S. degree from South China University of Technology, Guangzhou, China, in 2026. I am currently an MSc student at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, Sweden.

My research interests include AI for EDA, graph neural networks, hardware acceleration, and Deep Learning. I am particularly interested in how machine learning methods can be applied to circuit and system-level optimization problems.

I am currently building my background in Machine Learning for EDA, Deep Learning accelerator, and hardware-oriented system design, with a strong interest in future research on AI accelerators and EDA-related topics.

If you would like to connect or discuss related topics, feel free to drop me an email.

  • Ongoing work aims to utilize these computing formats in the Quamba hardware accelerator.

Bachelor’s thesis on the Design and Approximate Computation Methods of Quamba-based SSM & Linear Projection Hardware Accelerators Stockholm, Sweden
Bachelor’s thesis Dec 2025 - May 2026

  • Built a Docker + CUDA environment and successfully ran Quamba on a GPU, becoming familiar with its SSM (State Space Model) and Linear Projection computation flow for later hardware implementation.
  • Designed and implemented matrix accumulation, state update, and linear transformation units for FP16, FP32, and INT8 on FPGA.
  • Generated deterministic inputs and expected outputs using Python to construct hardware verification test cases, then loaded data onto hardware and restarted computation to ensure the FP16 unit outputs were consistent with Python benchmarks.
  • Tested post-layout simulation results and checked whether parameters still satisfied the requirements.
  • Incorporated optional approximation strategies into the hardware units and analyzed the impact of different approximation strategies on intermediate results and final output.
  • Compared hardware FP16, FP32, INT8, and approximate calculation results, and evaluated error indicators such as SSE and MSE.

Course Project of ASIC & FPGA Guangzhou, CHN
Student researcher April 2025 - June 2025

  • Designed and implemented a classical Miller-compensated two-stage CMOS operational amplifier, including bias network, differential input stage, and gain stage.
  • Performed top-down architecture selection and bottom-up transistor-level sizing to simultaneously satisfy gain, bandwidth, stability, and power constraints.

  • Performed full DRC, LVS, and PEX, and verified performance degradation after parasitic extraction.
